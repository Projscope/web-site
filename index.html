What I own

Three pillars of the engagement

01

Architecture & Risk Judgment

I make the technology decisions that are expensive to reverse, and I name the risks before they become incidents — sound calls on what to build, what to buy, and what to leave alone.

02

Engineering Leadership & Standards

I set the bar for how software gets built — code quality, delivery predictability, accountability — and I raise the level of the people doing it. Leadership isn't just direction; it's the standard everyone calibrates to.

03

The Translation Layer

I make technical reality legible to the business and business priorities legible to engineering — the calm layer that keeps each side focused, aligned, and moving on exactly what matters.

My career has spanned the full range — from the scrappy urgency of a lean startup, where one decision reshapes the whole company, to a large telecom, where leadership means aligning many teams across time zones, languages, and cultures. I've built and led engineering in multi-zone, multi-cultural environments where the human problems — translation, trust, and coordination between people who bring different languages and perspectives — matter as much as the technical ones. I've learned to solve for both.

That range is the point. I know what “good” looks like at scale, and how to get a small team there with the focus and speed that a lean stage rewards. I've sat in the founder's chair and in the enterprise boardroom — so I can read both rooms, and speak to both honestly.
